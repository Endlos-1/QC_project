AGENT FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

When should I consider taking a commiission advance?
Quick Commission Advance provides responsible, short term commission advances to help Realtors who are experiencing cash-flow challenges. We do this without running credit checks.

Who is eligible for commission advances?
Quick Commission Advance serves licensed residential real estate agents who are affiliated with leading real estate companies across the United States.

What is the maximum amount you will advance?
The maximum amount we will advance is $15,000.

How do you pay my advance to me?
We will deposit your deposit directly into your bank account.

How long does it take to receive my commission advance?
Typically, we'll provide same day approvals. In this instance, you will receive your advance within 24 hours after your commission advance is approved.

What types of contracts qualify foe a commission advance?
Commissions from the sale of single-family residential properties, including town homes qualify for a commission advance.

At waht stage of the transaction can I advance my commission?
We will advance you up to 80% of your commission.

Why only 30 days to close?
Closings greater than 30 days carry significantly high risk. In order to provide advances quickly, with no credit checks or lengthy underwriting timeframes, we limit closings to 30 days.

Do you credit checks?
No, we do not perform credit checks.

Will my request for a commission advance be confidential?
Your commission advance transaction will be kept confidential between your broker and escrow adgent. We do not sell our customer's information.

Do you contact the Buyer or Seller?
The buyer and or seller will not be contacted notified the transactions by us.

Does my broker need to register with Quick Commisssion?
No, brokers do not have to register with QUick Commission, however, they are required to approve each advance request that an agent makes because all commissions ar legally owned by the broker. Therefore, we will not advance your commission without your broker's consent.

Is there liability for my broker?
No, your broker does not bear any risk with a commission advance. You are solely responsible for the repayment of the commission advance. If the transaction fails through, you are responsible for replacing it with future earned commissions. The broker is responsible for directing any future earned commissions by the agent to Quick Commission Advance.

Can I have multiple advances at the same time?
Yes, as long as you do not exceed advance limit of $15,000, you may take more than one advance at a time. Individual and multiple advances are subject to and limited by the underwriting process.

Do you have advance on short sales?
No, we dont provide advances on a short sales.

Are there any application fees,wire transfer, or holding fees?
There only is the advance fees described in the aggreement.

What happens if the sale fall through?
A commission advance should only be taken responsibly, when you have other means to repay the advance in the unlikely event the sale were to cancel. This can be done simply by paying it off in full or in part, or by sending us a replacement contract to cover the canceled sale. If you choose to replace a canceled sale, you will be charges a new fee based on your original contract's advance fees.

Is there a minimum advance fees?
Minimum advance fee is $400.

How is Quick Commission Advance repayed?
The settlemet company directs payment of the applicable funds to Quick Commission Advance when the your sale closes. Your broker signs a disbursement authorization directing them to direct the portion of your commission purchased to Quick Commission Advance.
